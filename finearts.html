"My work is an exploration of the Pnar identity—bridging the gap between the oral traditions of the past and the visual language of the present. Through lithography and moving prints, I seek to capture the echoes of our ancestors."

— Ohiwan Lamar, Visual Arts Scholar

The Spirit of Thadlaskein

Stone Lithography | 24 x 36 Inches | 2025

A visual interpretation of the sacred lake, exploring the duality of calmness and the depth of history submerged beneath the surface. The textures represent the erosion of memory over time.

Echoes of the monoliths

Woodcut Relief Print | 18 x 24 Inches | 2024

Standing tall against the test of time, the monoliths of Nartiang serve as the silent guardians of our tribe. This piece uses high-contrast relief to emphasize their endurance.

Faces of the Harvest

Charcoal & Ink on Paper | A1 Size | 2025

Captured during the Behdeinkhlam festival, this series focuses on the micro-expressions of the dancers—the exhaustion and the ecstasy of spiritual fulfillment.
